Inside Look At My Logo Design

From the Beginning



Initially this started out as a project for my Visual Foundations Studios class and the letters L U V N represented a made up virtual reality company. Therefore, I tried to incorporate some way of that VR feel by using a headset. The first logo design (the one with the open cube) was mean to add a form of depth perception. The reason I left the box open was to show how VR is still in development and its future is open to changes. I also tried to play around a bit with trying to incorporate and combine the letters. In the logo with the cursive writing, it might be easy to see the lowercase L and U. Now imagine turning the whole thing upside down, you are able to see the V and N. This was a stretch but I was still in my early phases and wanted to explore multiple avenues.

And What Came Next



For my second draft I took 3 logo designs I liked and wanted to pursue further. For the open ended cube I took it in two directions. One direction looked to see what would happen if I tried to make the angles perfect and have the cube give off a sense of structure with thicker lines. The other route I took was to incorporate this randomness and messy feel. I completely exaggerated the bottom half of the cube while having it contrast against the top half. I like the way I was incorporating letters with the different shades and wanted to see of other ways in which I could accomplish this. As for the design with the letters, I played around with the typeface and the object I placed between the letters. I have included a few of the logo designs I developed from this draft in the slide show for this section.

Where I Ended Up



For my third draft, I decided to continue down the path of manipulating the letters. Eventually after some trials, I found a shaping I liked for letters. Somewhere along the way, I decided to use the eraser tool and strike a random line to represent the disruption that VR causes, as it is something outside the current norms. I decided to an additional strike through the letters and then started to render some parts of letters to be above or below these strikes. This added an additional layer of depth perception. After presenting it to my class, I now notice how the N is very difficult to see at the smaller scale and easily gets lost.

Moving Forward

While I was working on my second draft, I designed a really cool logo. However, I did not decide to move forward with this logo design because of how different it was from the others. Looking ahead, when I find time to revisit this logo project, I am going to work on this design and see where it takes me.
